About the Temple

The Hadimba Temple, also known as Hidimba Devi Temple, is located in Manali, Himachal Pradesh, India. Nestled amidst dense cedar forests at the foot of the Himalayas, this unique temple is dedicated to Hidimba Devi, wife of Bhima (one of the Pandava brothers from Mahabharata).

Famous for its unique pagoda-style wooden architecture with intricate carvings, the temple stands as an architectural marvel and an important pilgrimage site. The temple is surrounded by beautiful pine and cedar forests, making it a popular tourist destination.

Fun Facts

  • The temple does not have an idol of Hidimba but instead features a footprint on a rock believed to be in the form of the goddess.
  • The structure is made of wood and stone with a unique four-tiered pagoda roof, showcasing traditional Himachali architecture.
  • Built in 1553 CE by Maharaja Bahadur Singh, the temple stands as a testament to ancient craftsmanship.
  • The temple is surrounded by the Dhungiri Van Vihar, a beautiful cedar forest that adds to its serene atmosphere.
  • Yaks and rabbits are often found nearby for tourist rides and photos, adding to the unique experience.
  • The local Dhungari Festival held in May draws large crowds and showcases traditional Himachali culture and traditions.
  • The temple's wooden carvings feature intricate designs depicting gods, goddesses, and animals from Hindu mythology.

History

The Hadimba Temple was built in 1553 CE by Maharaja Bahadur Singh. It is dedicated to Hidimba Devi, the wife of Bhima, one of the five Pandava brothers from the epic Mahabharata. According to legend, Hidimba performed penance at this spot and the temple was built to honor her devotion.

The temple stands on a rock believed to be in the form of the goddess Hidimba. The unique architecture reflects the traditional pagoda-style construction prevalent in Himachal Pradesh during the 16th century.

Hidimba was a demoness who fell in love with Bhima when the Pandavas were in exile. After marrying Bhima, she gave birth to Ghatotkacha, who played a crucial role in the Mahabharata war. After Bhima left, Hidimba meditated in the forests of Manali and eventually attained divinity.

The temple has been recognized as an important heritage site and pilgrimage destination. Despite its age, the wooden structure has been remarkably well-preserved, showcasing the skill of ancient craftsmen. The temple continues to be an active place of worship and a major tourist attraction in Manali.

Visiting Information

The Hadimba Temple is located in Manali, Himachal Pradesh, approximately 2 km from the main town. Nestled amidst dense cedar forests at the foot of the Himalayas, it offers a peaceful and scenic environment for visitors and devotees alike.

Location: Dhungiri Van Vihar, Manali, Himachal Pradesh 175131. The temple is situated about 2 kilometers from Manali Mall Road.

How to Reach:

By Air: The nearest airport is Bhuntar Airport (Kullu-Manali Airport), approximately 50 km from Manali. Regular taxi services and buses connect the airport to Manali.

By Train: The nearest major railway station is Joginder Nagar (about 165 km away). From there, you can hire taxis or take buses to reach Manali.

By Road: Manali is well-connected by road from major cities. Regular buses run from Delhi (about 540 km), Chandigarh (310 km), and other nearby cities. Private taxis are also available.

Temple Timings: The temple is open daily from 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM. However, timings may vary during winter months due to heavy snowfall.

Best Time to Visit: April to July (summer) and September to October (autumn) offer pleasant weather. The temple is also beautiful during winter (December-February) when covered in snow, though accessibility may be limited.

Entry Fee: There is no entry fee to visit the temple, though donations are welcome.

Special Events: The Dhungari Festival held in May is a major attraction, featuring traditional dances, music, and cultural performances.
