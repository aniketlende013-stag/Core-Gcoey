About the Temple

Trimbakeshwar Temple, located near Nashik in Maharashtra, is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. This sacred site holds immense spiritual significance, with devotees visiting to seek blessings and spiritual solace.

Fun Facts

  • Trimbakeshwar houses one of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva.
  • The temple features a unique architecture with three lingams representing Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva.
  • It is located near the source of the Godavari River.
  • Devotees often perform rituals and holy baths at nearby sacred ponds.
  • The temple sees thousands of visitors during Mahashivratri.

History

Trimbakeshwar Temple is an ancient shrine believed to be built during the 18th century. It is famous for its architectural marvels and spiritual significance.

The temple is known for its three-faced linga representing the Hindu Trinity: Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva. Pilgrims consider visiting Trimbakeshwar as highly auspicious and spiritually rewarding.

The Godavari River originates near this temple, making it a significant pilgrimage site. The temple management oversees various rituals, festivals, and charitable activities throughout the year.

Visiting Information

Trimbakeshwar is easily accessible by road and rail, with Nashik being the nearest major city.

By Train: Nashik Road Railway Station connects Trimbakeshwar with major cities like Mumbai and Pune.

By Road: State and private buses operate regularly from Nashik and nearby towns. The temple is approximately 28 km from Nashik.

Accommodation: Nearby guesthouses and dharamshalas cater to pilgrims.

Best Time to Visit: October to February for pleasant weather; Mahashivratri is the main festival.

Temple Timings: 5:30 AM to 8:30 PM daily; special timings during festivals.
