About Raigad Fort

Raigad Fort is situated in the Sahyadri mountain range near Mahad in Maharashtra. It is a historic hill fort known for its strategic importance and architecture. Once the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, it represents Maratha valor and resilience.

Key Facts

  • Elevation: About 1,356 meters above sea level
  • Original Name: Rairi
  • Trek: 1,737 steps to reach the fort summit
  • Ropeway: Available for convenient access with scenic views
  • Capital Period: Maratha Empire capital from 1674
  • Best Time to Visit: August to mid-October

Historical Significance

Originally known as Rairi, the fort was captured by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1656 from Chandrarao More of Jawali. He renovated it into a stronghold and in 1674 made Raigad the capital of his empire.

Coronations at Raigad: Three historic coronations took place here - Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj in 1674, his son Sambhaji Maharaj in 1681, and his younger son Rajaram Maharaj in 1689.

British Era: After 1818, the British took control of the fort, and it gradually lost its strategic importance. However, its historical significance as the birthplace of the Maratha Empire remains undiminished.

Recent Discoveries: An astrolabe dating back to 1597 was found near Kushavarta Lake. This copper-bronze instrument has Sanskrit inscriptions and animal engravings, highlighting the advanced scientific knowledge of Shivaji Maharaj's era.

The fort stands as a testament to Maratha engineering prowess and strategic vision, representing the glorious chapter of Indian history when indigenous rulers successfully challenged foreign dominance.

Architectural Highlights

Raigad Fort showcases excellent Maratha architecture built using basalt, granite, and lime mortar.

Maha Darwaja

The main entrance with two massive bastions that served as the primary gateway to the fort.

Nagarkhana

The drum house where royal announcements were made to inform the people of important events.

Rajwada

The royal palace complex built in traditional Maratha architectural style for the royal family.

Jagdishwar Temple

A beautiful Shiva temple built in the distinctive Hemadpanthi architectural style.

Ganga Sagar Lake

A rock-hewn water tank that was used in coronation rituals and ceremonies.

Hirakani Buruj

A bastion named after Hirakani, a woman who scaled the dangerous cliffs to reach her child.

Visiting Information

Raigad Fort is accessible by both trekking and ropeway. The fort offers stunning panoramic views of the Sahyadri mountain ranges and is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts and adventure seekers.

August to mid-October when the surroundings are lush green and weather is pleasant for trekking.

How to Reach: The fort is located near Mahad in Raigad district. Mahad is well-connected by road from Mumbai (approximately 170 km) and Pune (approximately 120 km). Regular bus services are available.

Entry Timings: The fort is open from 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM. It's advisable to start early to avoid crowds and have ample time to explore.

Entry Fee: Nominal entry fee for Indians and higher fee for foreign nationals. Ropeway has separate charges.

What to Carry: Water bottles, comfortable trekking shoes, hat or cap, camera, and light snacks. The trek can be demanding, so proper preparation is essential.

Nearby Attractions: Rajgad Fort (another prominent Maratha fort), Kondhane Buddhist Caves (ancient rock-cut caves), and various trekking trails in the Sahyadri range.
