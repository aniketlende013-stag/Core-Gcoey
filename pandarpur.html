About the Temple

The Vitthal Temple in Pandharpur, located on the banks of the Chandrabhaga (Bhima) River in Maharashtra, is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in India. It is dedicated to Lord Vitthal (Vithoba), considered a form of Lord Vishnu/Krishna, along with his consort Rukmini. The temple is renowned for its unique deity posture - standing on a brick with hands on hips.

Fun Facts

  • The temple was built during the period of Hoysala king Vishnuvardhana between 1108–1152 CE.
  • Lord Vitthal's unique posture - standing on a brick with hands on hips - is found nowhere else.
  • The name "Vitthal" is derived from "vit + thal" meaning "one who stands".
  • Pandharpur is one of the 108 Abhimana Kshetras in the Vaishnavite tradition.
  • The famous Warkari pilgrimage brings millions of devotees walking hundreds of kilometers.
  • During Ashadhi and Kartiki Ekadashi, over 10 lakh devotees visit the temple.

History & Legend

The temple has a rich history dating back to the 12th century CE. The most famous legend tells of devotee Pundalik who was serving his parents when Lord Krishna visited. Pundalik asked the Lord to wait and placed a brick for him to stand on, leading to the unique standing posture of Vitthal.

The temple was built during the Hoysala period between 1108–1152 CE under king Vishnuvardhana. However, inscriptions suggest the site was already significant for Vishnu worship in earlier centuries.

According to legend, when Lord Krishna came to visit the devoted Pundalik, who was busy serving his elderly parents, Pundalik threw a brick and asked the Lord to stand and wait. Pleased by Pundalik's devotion to his parents, Krishna stood on the brick with hands on his waist, symbolizing his readiness to help devotees while honoring family devotion.

The temple is built in the Deccan architectural style with Hoysala influences, featuring massive halls, ornate gateways, and exquisite stone carvings. The deity Vitthal is worshipped along with his consort Rukmini in separate shrines within the complex.

Visiting Information

Pandharpur is located in Solapur district, Maharashtra, on the banks of the sacred Chandrabhaga River. The temple is well-connected by road, rail, and air, making it accessible for pilgrims from across India.

By Train: Pandharpur Railway Station is well connected to major cities including Mumbai, Pune, Solapur, and other important destinations across Maharashtra and India.

By Air: Nearest airport is Pune (approximately 210-215 km away). Regular taxi and bus services are available from the airport to Pandharpur.

By Road: MSRTC buses regularly ply from Mumbai, Pune, Solapur, Kolhapur and other major cities. Private buses and taxis are also available.

Temple Timings: The temple opens early morning around 4-6 AM and closes late at night around 11:30 PM to midnight, with darshan available in multiple sessions throughout the day.

Special Festivals: During Ashadhi Ekadashi (June-July) and Kartiki Ekadashi (Oct-Nov), the temple remains open 24 hours with special arrangements for the massive influx of Warkari pilgrims.

Accommodation: Various dharmashalas, guest houses, and hotels are available in Pandharpur to suit different budgets. Advance booking is recommended during festival periods.

Best Time to Visit: November to February offers pleasant weather with comfortable temperatures for pilgrimage. Avoid visiting during peak summer months.
