About the Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Sri Harmandir Sahib or Darbar Sahib, is the most sacred gurdwara of the Sikh religion. Located in Amritsar, Punjab, India, its upper floors are covered with 750 kg of gold, giving it its renowned name. Surrounded by the Amrit Sarovar (holy water tank), believed to have healing powers, the temple welcomes people of all religions, castes, and backgrounds.

Fun Facts

  • The Golden Temple receives over 1 lakh (100,000) visitors daily, making it one of the most visited places in the world.
  • It has four entrances in all directions, symbolizing openness and acceptance of people from all walks of life.
  • The upper floors are covered with 750 kg of pure gold, giving the temple its distinctive golden appearance.
  • The temple runs the world's largest free community kitchen (Langar), serving up to 1 lakh people daily regardless of religion, caste, or background.
  • The holy book, Guru Granth Sahib, is ceremoniously carried to the Akal Takht every night and brought back in the morning.
  • The temple complex includes the Akal Takht, the highest seat of Sikh temporal authority.
  • The Amrit Sarovar (holy water tank) surrounding the temple is believed to have healing properties.

History

The Golden Temple was founded in 1581 by Guru Arjan Dev Ji, the 5th Sikh Guru. Construction was completed in 1604, when the Guru Granth Sahib (the holy scripture) was placed inside for the first time.

The foundation of the temple was laid by a Muslim saint, Mian Mir, at the request of Guru Arjan Dev Ji, symbolizing the temple's message of universal brotherhood and religious harmony.

In the early 19th century, Maharaja Ranjit Singh, the Sikh ruler of Punjab, rebuilt and beautified the temple with gold plating, giving it the magnificent appearance we see today. The gold leaf work was completed between 1830 and 1860.

Throughout history, the temple has faced several attacks and reconstructions, but it has always remained the spiritual and cultural center of Sikhism. The temple stands as a symbol of resilience, faith, and the eternal message of equality and service to humanity.

Visiting Information

The Golden Temple is located in Amritsar, Punjab, India. It is well-connected by road, rail, and air, making it easily accessible for pilgrims from around the world. Entry is free for all, and the temple welcomes people of all religions, castes, and backgrounds.

By Train: Amritsar Railway Station is well connected to major cities across India including Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, and Chennai. The temple is about 2 km from the station.

By Air: Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport is about 11 km from the temple with regular flights from major Indian cities and some international destinations.

By Road: Excellent bus services from Delhi (450 km), Chandigarh (230 km), and other cities. State transport and private buses regularly operate to Amritsar.

Temple Timings: The temple remains open 24 hours a day, all year round. The best times to visit are early morning (sunrise) and evening when the temple is beautifully illuminated.

Dress Code: Covering the head is mandatory for all visitors. Head scarves are available at the entrance. Remove shoes before entering and maintain modest dress.

Langar (Free Kitchen): Open to all visitors 24/7. Experience the community dining where everyone sits together on the floor, symbolizing equality.

Accommodation: Free accommodation is available at the temple complex on a first-come, first-served basis. Many hotels and guesthouses are also available nearby for all budgets.

Best Time to Visit: October to March offers pleasant weather. Morning (sunrise) and evening (when the temple is illuminated and reflects in the Sarovar) are the most beautiful times.
